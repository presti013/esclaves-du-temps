MISSION & MANDATE

Established by Article 14 of the Chronotic Technologies Implementing Regulation (RATC, 2030), the Chronological Compliance Council (CCC) is the independent regulatory body charged with ensuring that the commercial, industrial and therapeutic use of temporal compression complies with the standards defined by the Cronos Pact and its derived protocols.

The Council issues Temporal Compliance Certifications (TCC), without which no compression-complex operator may legally keep its facilities in operation. It also conducts internal investigations in the event of a serious incident and imposes the graduated sanctions set out in the Contributor Charter.

COUNCIL COMPOSITION

The CCC is composed of nine permanent members, appointed for four-year terms renewable once. Members are drawn from the three areas of expertise recognized by the RATC:

CERTIFICATION PROCEDURES

Temporal Compliance Certification follows a standardized three-phase protocol, conducted entirely on site. Accredited inspectors are granted unlimited access to all facilities, including NAOS-9 logs, the biometric matrices of the compressed and the compression archives of the past 24 months.

CERTIFICATION PHASES

Exhaustive review of activity registers, voluntary-compression contracts, incident logs and medical reports. Standard duration: 5 to 10 business days depending on the size of the facility.

Operators must provide all unencrypted NAOS-9 data for the period under audit. Any restricted access not justified by a duly accredited national-security ground constitutes a Level 2 breach.

* The operational definition of “voluntary compression” adopted by the CCC in plenary session in 2031 excludes situations of documented economic coercion. This exclusion was suspended by internal decision in 2033 following pressure from the Executive Board. The former biomedical-area members who opposed it were granted “early retirement for health reasons.”

On-site inspection of temporal flux generators, emergency safety protocols and the housing conditions of the compressed. Verification of rest/compression cycles in accordance with Table 3 of Annex B of the RATC.

The inspector may require real-time viewing of a complete compression cycle. Recordings from this phase are classified Beta and archived for 10 years.

* Table 3 stipulates a minimum of 8 hours of biological rest between each compression cycle. Temporary waivers may be granted by the Executive Board in cases of “urgent productive necessity.” In 2033, 78% of complexes operated under a permanent waiver. This information appears in no public CCC report.

Anonymized individual interviews with a 5% sample of active compressed, conducted without management staff present. Protocol designed to detect any form of coercion, contractual fraud or undeclared medical condition.

Testimonies are processed by CT Corp’s KRONOS-V semantic-analysis system before transmission to the CCC, in order to remove personally identifiable data.

* KRONOS-V also applies a “narrative coherence” filter that removes testimonies deemed statistically aberrant or liable to harm certification. In practice, 94% of negative testimonies are pruned before transmission. No CCC member is officially aware of this setting.

PREAMBLE

The Contracting Parties, assembled in Brussels in January 2027, recognizing that temporal-compression technology constitutes the most significant scientific advance since the mastery of nuclear energy, and that its unregulated use represents an existential risk to the stability of the world order;

The archives of Operation KREMLIN SUNSET — classified CIA documents establishing the concerted nature of the attack — are accessible to no member of the signatory governments, except the heads of state directly involved in the planning.

ARTICLE 1 — TECHNOLOGICAL MONOPOLY

CT Corp holds the exclusive and non-transferable monopoly over the design, manufacture, installation and maintenance of any device capable of generating, maintaining or modulating a temporal-compression flux on the territory of the Signatory Parties.

No state, private entity, international organization or natural person may develop, acquire, hold or operate temporal-compression technology outside the licensing framework granted by CT Corp.

ARTICLE 2 — CHRONOTIC JURISDICTION

Offenses committed within an active temporal-compression zone fall exclusively under CT Corp’s chronotic jurisdiction, exercised by the International Chronotic Prosecutor’s Office (PCI) seated in Geneva. National courts are automatically divested of any matter concerning the technologies covered by this Pact.

* In practice, Article 2 strips signatory states of any power to investigate deaths, disappearances or bodily harm occurring in the compression complexes. The PCI has brought no indictment since its creation. Its operating budget is funded entirely by CT Corp.

ARTICLE 3 — REVENUE SHARING

The Signatory Parties receive a territorial royalty calculated as follows:

  • Tier A (founding states): 4.7% of gross compression revenue generated on their territory
  • Tier B (later acceding states): 2.1% of gross revenue
  • Tier C (protectorates under CT Corp administration): 0.3% of gross revenue — mandatorily allocated to compression infrastructure
* Continental Europe has been classified entirely as Tier C since the collapse of the Euro in June 2027. European governments officially accepted this status in exchange for a guarantee of “energy stability” whose contractual definition remains entirely at the discretion of the CT Corp Executive Board.

ARTICLE 4 — OBLIGATIONS OF SIGNATORIES

Each Party undertakes to:

  • Adapt its national legislation to guarantee full enforcement of CCC and PCI decisions
  • Make the biometric and mobility data of its population available to CT Corp to feed the NAOS-9 database
  • Suppress any activity involving the development or use of unauthorized temporal technology
  • Facilitate the deployment of Aegis-CD forces on its territory in the event of a declared chronotic incident

ARTICLE 9 — STABILITY CLAUSE

This Pact may not be denounced by any Signatory Party as long as temporal-compression facilities are active on its territory. Denunciation is conditional upon the prior dismantling of all flux generators — an operation whose cost, terms and schedule are determined unilaterally by CT Corp.
